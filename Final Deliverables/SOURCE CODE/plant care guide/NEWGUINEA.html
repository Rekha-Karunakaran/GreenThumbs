NEW GUINEA IMPATIENS


Growing New Guinea impatiens in containers allows you to enjoy a big show of bright blooms just about anywhere. Brighten up a kitchen windowsill, sunroom or patio with these non-stop flowering plants.You'll find them in shades of pink, red, mauve, purple, orange and white.

CARING TIPS


Temperature:

Average room temperatures 60-75°F/16-24°C.


Light:

Bright light; no direct sun in summer. Impatiens won't bloom if they don't get enough light. When growing impatiens indoors, it can be challenging to find a spot where they'll get at least 4 hours of bright, indirect sun each day.


Watering:

Keep the soil evenly moist, but not soggy. Flowering plants are thirsty, and they dry out quickly in containers, so check them often. Impatiens will quickly wilt if they are allowed to dry out. They can be quickly revived though with a thorough watering.


Soil:

Peat moss-based potting mix with added perlite and/or vermiculite for faster drainage. African violet potting mix is ideal for impatiens.


Humidity:

Aim for 40% relative humidity or higher.
