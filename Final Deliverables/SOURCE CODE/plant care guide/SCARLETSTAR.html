SCARLET STAR


COMMON NAME: Scarlet star

The Scarlet Star is a popular variety of plants that can grow outdoors or indoors when attached to another plant.They begin as a light green color with a soft feel, and mature into leathery, deep green leaves. When viewed from above, the entire flower head takes on the shape of a star, giving the plant its name.

CARING TIPS


Temperature:

The Scarlet Star prefers an average temperature of 65-80°F (18-27°C).


Light:

This plant prefers bright, yet indirect sunlight all times. Too much sunlight will cause the leaves to sunburn and damage the flower.


Watering:

The cup of the flower must be kept filled with 1 to 2 inches of water at all times. Every four days, pour the old water out of the cup and provide new water. Never use anything except distilled or rain-sourced water, as the chemicals in processed water will kill the plant.


Soil:

The plant requires a mixture of one part soil to one part pumice or bark to thrive.


Humidity:

The Scarlet Star demands humid living conditions. If you keep your home at average air humidity, spray the leaves and flower cup with a light dose of water every other day.
