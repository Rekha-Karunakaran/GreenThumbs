MADAGASCAR JASMINE


COMMON NAME: Wax flower

The Madagascar Jasmine is an attractive climbing vine species, grown outdoors and indoors for its clusters of scented blooms and shiny oval-shaped leaves.

CARING TIPS


Temperature:

Temperatures from 65 – 80°F (18 – 26°C) are ideal during spring and summer. Around 55 – 60°F (13 – 15°C) for a month or two during winter.


Light:

Plenty of bright light is needed during the active growing season.A spot close to a window with the right balance of sunlight and shade is ideal.


Watering:

While the plant is actively growing you’ll need to water the plant thoroughly. Just top the water up slightly during the winter, only when the first top inch of soil becomes quite dry.


Soil:

A peat moss-based potting mix with 2 parts peat moss and 1 part perlite is suggested.


Humidity:

During the summer extra humidity may need to be provided if the air becomes dry. Avoid dry air from artificial heating during the winter.
