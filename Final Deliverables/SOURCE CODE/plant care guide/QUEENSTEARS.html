QUEEN'S TEARS


Queen's Tears is not as easy to find in nurseries as its brome relatives. However, you'll find it's easier to please than some and is worth growing.Its thick, strappy leaves arch outward into a fountain shape, making this tropical beauty a graceful accent plant.

Light:

Bright, indirect light. If you move your billbergia outdoors for the summer, keep it in partial shade. Keep an eye on it so the leaves don't turn red or get scorched from too much sun.


Watering:

Use room-temperature rainwater or distilled water because this rainforest native is sensitive to chlorine and fluoride added to some tap water.


Soil:

Bromeliad or orchid mix is chunky -- ideal fast drainage and also allows air around this bromeliad's roots


Humidity:

Try to maintain 40-50% relative humidity or higher.
